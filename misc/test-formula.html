Given $$ \begin{align} i_1 = [l_1, u_1] \\ i_2 = [l_2, u_2] \end{align} $$ We can get $$ \begin{align} p_1 = \frac{u_1 - l_1}{u_1 + l_1} \\ p_2 = \frac{u_2 - l_2}{u_2 + l_2} \\ \end{align} $$ Assume all numbers are positinve $$ i_3 = i_1i_2 = [l_1l_2, u_1u_2] $$ So $$ p_3 = \frac{u_1u_2 - l_1l_2}{u_1u_2 + l_1l_2} $$ Since $$ \begin{align} p_1 &= \frac{u_1 - l_1}{u_1 + l_1} \\ &= \frac{1 - \frac{l_1}{u_1}}{1 + \frac{l_1}{u_1}} \\ \end{align} $$ We can get $$ \begin{align} \frac{l_1}{u_1} = \frac{1 - p_1}{1 + p_1} \\ \frac{l_2}{u_2} = \frac{1 - p_2}{1 + p_2} \end{align} $$ So $$ \begin{align} p_3 &= \frac{u_1u_2 - l_1l_2}{u_1u_2 + l_1l_2} \\ &= \frac{1 - \frac{l_1}{u_1}\frac{l_2}{u_2}}{1 + \frac{l_1}{u_1}\frac{l_2}{u_2}} \\ &= \frac{1 - \frac{1 - p_1}{1 + p_1}\frac{1 - p_2}{1 + p_2}}{1 + \frac{1 - p_1}{1 + p_1}\frac{1 - p_2}{1 + p_2}} \\ &= \frac{p_1 + p_2}{1 + p_1p_2} \\ &\approx p_1 + p_2 \end{align} $$
